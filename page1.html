Calculating Dirt-loading Cycles by Deep Learning Detector and Kalman Filter    

Abstract:

Earth moving work is one of the most important activities in construction sites. It is time and labor consuming to estimate productivity manually during earth moving operations. In this research, a hybrid method has been proposed to detect and track construction equipment automatically from video stream in order to calculate dirt-loading cycles. By using this method, the productivities of excavators and trucks could be estimated precisely. In this research, deep learning detector tiny-YOLO has been trained for detecting trucks, excavator buckets, and excavator cabs individually. Once these objects have been detected, Kalman filter method will be employed for tracking each target. Then, the loading cycles can be calculated according to the pixel positions between excavators and trucks. A preliminary study has been conducted in real construction sites, which has gained good performance in accuracy. The future research will be focused on optimizing this method on embedded system and enable it works in real-time.
